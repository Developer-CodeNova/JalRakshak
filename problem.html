The Problem

Why water management is broken today

The Real Problem

Water scarcity is not only caused by lack of rainfall. It is mainly caused by poor management of available water.

In most homes, schools and villages, people manually operate water motors. They often forget to switch them off, causing tank overflow and massive wastage.

Hidden Losses

Leakages inside walls or underground pipelines remain unnoticed for months. This results in continuous water loss and structural damage to buildings.

Dry running of pumps occurs when motors run without water. This damages motors, increases electricity bills, and shortens the life of expensive equipment.

Health Risk

Stored water becomes contaminated over time. People drink dirty water without knowing, leading to diseases such as diarrhoea and infections.

There is currently no simple system that monitors water quality in real time.

Core Issue

The real problem is not technology. The real problem is that smart technology has not reached common people.

That is the gap JalRakshak is designed to fill.
